Sinhagad Fort, Pune - Travel Guide

Last Updated: July, 2024


Sinhagad Fort, situated about 35 kilometers from Pune in Maharashtra, India, stands as a testament to the region's rich history and natural beauty. Perched atop a hill in the Sahyadri mountain range at an altitude of about 1,312 meters (4,304 feet), the fort offers breathtaking views of the surrounding valleys and landscapes, making it a favorite destination for history buffs and nature lovers alike. The origins of Sinhagad Fort can be traced back to around the 12th century. Originally known as "Kondhana," the fort has changed hands several times over the centuries, with its most famous battle occurring in 1670. This battle was led by the Maratha warrior Tanaji Malusare, a close aide of Chatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Tanaji and his troops launched a daring midnight attack on the fort to reclaim it from the Mughals. The battle was fierce, and despite losing his life, Tanaji's efforts led to a Maratha victory, after which Shivaji Maharaj famously remarked, "Gad ala, pan Sinha gela" ("The fort is won, but the lion is lost"). In honor of Tanaji’s bravery, the fort was renamed Sinhagad, or "Lion's Fort."


Today, Sinhagad Fort is a popular trekking destination, offering trails that are both challenging and rewarding. The trek to the fort is particularly popular during the monsoon season when the entire region comes alive with lush greenery and cascading waterfalls. The fort itself has several points of interest, including the Pune Gate and Kalyan Gate, ancient bastions, and the memorial of Tanaji Malusare. Visitors can explore the ruins and immerse themselves in the fort's history while enjoying panoramic views of the landscape below.


In addition to its historical significance, Sinhagad Fort is known for its local culinary delights. Numerous small stalls and eateries around the fort offer traditional Maharashtrian snacks such as "pithla bhakri," "kanda bhaji" (onion fritters), and refreshing buttermilk, which provide a taste of local culture. The fort remains a perfect blend of history, adventure, and natural beauty, making it a must-visit destination near Pune.
